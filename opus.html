Deep conditional generative models

NCN OPUS Project (2022/45/B/ST6/01117)

We aim at constructing conditional deep generative models, which allow for controlling the process of generating examples. In this project, we follow an emerging trend in deep learning, which relies on reusing well-established pre-trained models, which can be effectively adapted to diverse applications with a minimal conceptual and training effort. We plan to design lightweight plugin networks, we are attached to the latent space of the pre-trained models and allow for manipulating their latent codes. Typical way of modeling conditional distribution relies on parametrizing deep generative models with conditioning factors. In this project, we follow a different approach and model each conditioning factor using an individual subset of latent attributes. This can be seen as a way of disentangling the latent space of deep generative models, because we separate conditioning information from the remaining variables. The project assumes the following goals:
  • Building deep generative models for creating new samples (modifying existing examples) with the requested attributes.
  • Generating counterfactual explanations for decisions of deep learning models.
  • Generating chemical molecules and 3D shapes with the requested properties.
  • Solving inverse problems using deep generative models.
  • Comparison and analysis of various conditioning mechanisms in deep generative models.
